Quick answers

Questions Annie gets asked every week.

If your question isn't covered in the guides above, it's probably here. Tap a question to expand the answer.

Yes — Phuket is generally very safe for families. Road safety is the most relevant concern: most expat families with young kids drive cars rather than scooters, particularly for the school run. Day-to-day, neighbourhoods like Bangtao, Cherngtalay, and Kathu are quiet and low-risk. Petty theft exists, as anywhere, but violent crime against expats is genuinely rare.
No. English is widely spoken in the areas where most expats live — in shops, restaurants, hospitals, schools, and most of the services you'll use day-to-day. That said, learning even basic Thai changes how locals respond to you. It's not necessary, but a few months of casual lessons make life noticeably warmer.
For families with school-age children, 6–12 months is ideal — school enrollment and waiting lists need real lead time, especially for popular British and IB schools. For couples without children, or retirees, 3–6 months is usually enough. The visa is rarely the bottleneck; it's usually schools or shipping.
Almost always, yes. Renting for 6–12 months lets you experience an area through both seasons, work out your actual routine, and make a much more informed purchase decision. Buying off the back of a two-week scouting trip is the single most common regret we hear from expats five years in.
November through April is peak season — dry, sunny, and the easiest time to experience Phuket at its best. If you're seriously considering the move, also try to come in August or September during the monsoon. Rainy-season Phuket is a different place, and it's a much fairer test of how you'll handle it long-term.
Phuket strikes a balance: better international school options than Bali, a more grounded family scene than central Bangkok, and a slower pace than Chiang Mai's expat hub. The trade-off is cost — Phuket is the most expensive of the four, especially in Bangtao and Cherngtalay. We'd happily talk you out of Phuket if your situation actually fits one of the others better.
