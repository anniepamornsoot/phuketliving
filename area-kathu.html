Areas/Kathu

Area guide · Central Phuket

Kathu — the practical centre of the island.

Central, affordable, and well-positioned for families who don't need a beach view to feel settled. Kathu sits between the west coast and Phuket Town — five minutes to BIMS Hospital, fifteen to international schools, twenty to anywhere else. Rents are roughly half what you'd pay in Bangtao for the same space.

Annie's honest take

"Kathu is where I send families who want the schools and the hospitals without paying west-coast prices for them. It's not on a beach — that's the catch — but you're ten minutes from anywhere that matters. For value-conscious families, this is genuinely the smartest pick on the island."

Daily life

What living here actually feels like.

Not the brochure version. The actual rhythm of a Tuesday in Kathu — what you do, where you go, what's in walking distance and what isn't.

Morning routine

Coffee at one of the cafés along Patak Road, breakfast at a local Thai stall (which still outnumber the cafés), or a quick stop at the morning market for fresh fish and produce. Mornings here feel more Thai than expat — which is part of the appeal if you want to live in Phuket rather than parallel to it.

Schooling

The Bangtao school cluster is fifteen minutes north — slightly against the morning traffic, which keeps the commute predictable. BCIS, HeadStart, UWC, and Berda Claude are all within a twenty-minute window. Manageable for families who'd rather spend the saved rent on better schools or more travel.

Getting around

A car is essential — Kathu isn't really walkable to anywhere expat-relevant. The flip side: the central position means most journeys are short. Most families here run a single car plus a scooter for the school run and supermarket trips.

Healthcare

Healthcare is Kathu's strongest practical advantage. BIMS is five minutes away, Bangkok Hospital Phuket is twelve, and Phuket International Hospital is fifteen. For families with young children or elderly relatives visiting, this proximity is genuinely valuable — and difficult to match anywhere else on the island.

Weekends

Patong is ten minutes (a feature for some, a flaw for others). Karon and Kata beaches are fifteen to twenty. Phuket Town is twenty for markets, restaurants, and Sino-Portuguese architecture. Kathu Waterfall is on your doorstep, and Phuket Country Club is the island's most established golf course.

Nearest schools

School options from Kathu.

The honest picture

What Kathu gets right — and what it doesn't.

Every area has trade-offs. These are Kathu's, written plainly, so you can decide on the basis of what's actually true rather than what sounds good in a brochure.

What works well

  • Cheapest rents of the six areas — often half of Bangtao for the same square footage
  • Best healthcare access on the island — BIMS in 5 min, Bangkok Hospital Phuket in 12
  • Central position — 10 min to Patong, 15 to schools, 20 to Phuket Town
  • Growing expat community — not as established as Bangtao but adding fast
  • Genuinely local feel — strong Thai community, real markets, authentic food
  • Phuket Country Club golf course and Kathu Waterfall both nearby
  • Good for value-conscious families and remote workers who don't need a daily beach

What to consider

  • Not on the beach — Karon and Kata are 15–20 min by car
  • Patak Road can get traffic-heavy during school hours and high season
  • Less established expat community means fewer ready-made social networks
  • Patong's nightlife is close — relevant if you have teenagers
  • Some pockets of Kathu are residential or industrial — worth visiting before committing
  • Construction is ongoing as the area grows — check the immediate neighbours
  • Less aesthetically uniform than the master-planned developments further north
