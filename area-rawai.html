Areas/Rawai

Area guide · South Phuket

Rawai — quiet, local, genuinely Phuket.

If Bangtao feels like an expat suburb, Rawai feels like a town. It has a slower rhythm, a stronger local identity, and sea views that most areas of Phuket can't match. The trade-off is school distance — but for couples and families with older or pre-school children, it's often the most liveable area on the island.

Annie's honest take

"Rawai is for people who value peace over proximity. If schools aren't urgent, it's quietly the most liveable area in Phuket — you'll feel like you actually live somewhere, not like you're staying in a long-term resort."

Daily life

What living here actually feels like.

Not the brochure version. The actual rhythm of a Tuesday in Rawai — what you do, where you go, what's in walking distance and what isn't.

Morning routine

Coffee at the seafront before the heat sets in, fresh fish at the pier market, breakfast at one of the small Thai places along the main road. Very few tourists this early — it's genuinely local until late morning, especially mid-week.

Schooling

No international schools in Rawai itself — the closest are 15–20 minutes by car. Manageable with a school-run routine, but worth committing to before you sign a lease. Most Rawai families with school-age kids drive against the morning traffic, which keeps the journey predictable.

Getting around

You'll need a car or scooter. Rawai isn't really walkable to supermarkets — but Nai Harn beach, Chalong pier, and the main shopping strip are all within ten minutes. Most expat families here run a car for the school-run and a scooter for everything else.

Healthcare

Chalong Hospital is ten minutes away for routine care. Bangkok Hospital Phuket — the main private hospital on the island — is around twenty-five minutes by car. Adequate for day-to-day, with strong options for anything serious. Most expats here carry international cover and use the private system.

Weekends

Nai Harn beach for a swim before lunch, the Windmill viewpoint for sunset, Promthep Cape on a clear evening, or a dive trip from Chalong pier. Some of the best sunsets in Phuket are within a fifteen-minute radius — without the crowds you'll find further north.

The honest picture

What Rawai gets right — and what it doesn't.

Every area has trade-offs. These are Rawai's, written plainly, so you can decide on the basis of what's actually true rather than what sounds good in a brochure.

What works well

  • Some of the best sea views and sunsets in Phuket — Promthep, Nai Harn, the Windmill
  • Noticeably quieter than the north — you feel the difference the moment you arrive
  • Strong local market scene and authentic Thai restaurants, not just expat cafés
  • Lower rent than Bangtao for equivalent space, often by 20–30%
  • Growing expat community with a more grounded, less resort-y feel
  • Easy weekend access to diving, hiking, and the southern beaches

What to consider

  • No international schools in the area — a 15–20 minute daily drive is required
  • Not walkable — a car or scooter is essential, not optional
  • Fewer Western supermarkets and restaurants than Bangtao or Cherngtalay
  • Further from the airport (~45 min) — relevant if you travel frequently for work
  • Healthcare is good locally but the major hospital is 25 minutes north
  • Wet season can flood some lower streets briefly — worth checking the property's elevation
