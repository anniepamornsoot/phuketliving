Areas/Bangtao

Area guide · Northwest Phuket

Bangtao — where families settle quickly.

If Rawai feels like a town, Bangtao feels like a well-organised expat enclave. The most school-friendly area on the island, with a strong community already in place — and the trade-offs you'd expect from a place built around expat convenience.

Annie's honest take

"Bangtao is the family default for a reason. Three international schools within ten minutes, a beach in five, and you'll find your people in your first month. It's the least local of the six areas — but for families with school-age kids, that's often a feature, not a flaw."

Daily life

What living here actually feels like.

Not the brochure version. The actual rhythm of a Tuesday in Bangtao — what you do, where you go, what's in walking distance and what isn't.

Morning routine

Coffee at Café del Mar or one of the cafés along Soi Cherngtalay before school drop-off. The beach is a five-minute walk for an early swim, and the coffee culture is more international than Thai — for better or worse, depending on what you came here for.

Schooling

Bangtao is built around schooling. BCIS, HeadStart Bangtao, and UWC Thailand are all within fifteen minutes of most homes. The school run is the rhythm of the area — most families' day genuinely starts at 7:30am drop-off and ends at 3pm pickup, and you'll see the same parents at both.

Getting around

Mostly drivable, partially walkable — particularly within Laguna, which is golf-cart friendly and connected by paths. Outside Laguna, you'll need a car for serious errands. Most expat families here run two vehicles: a car for the school run and a scooter for Boat Avenue and the beach.

Healthcare

Bangkok Hospital Phuket is twenty minutes by car — the main private hospital on the island. BIMS is closer, and there are smaller clinics within Bangtao for everyday care. Healthcare access is one of Bangtao's strongest practical advantages, and a real factor for families with young kids or elderly relatives visiting.

Weekends

Bangtao Beach for swimming and the beach clubs (XANA, Catch). Laguna's lagoons for paddleboard and kid-friendly walks. Boat Avenue and Porto de Phuket for dinner and groceries. Many families don't leave the area on weekends — there's genuinely enough to fill them, which is the area's charm and also its limitation.

The honest picture

What Bangtao gets right — and what it doesn't.

Every area has trade-offs. These are Bangtao's, written plainly, so you can decide on the basis of what's actually true rather than what sounds good in a brochure.

What works well

  • Three international schools within ten minutes — unmatched anywhere else on the island
  • Strong, well-established expat community — easy to find your circle in the first month
  • Bangtao Beach is a five-minute walk from most homes
  • Bangkok Hospital Phuket and BIMS both within twenty minutes
  • Boat Avenue and Porto de Phuket cover dining and groceries without a long drive
  • More predictable weather, less monsoon flooding than the south coast

What to consider

  • Highest rents on the island for equivalent space — expect ฿20,000+ premium over Rawai
  • Most "expat suburb" feel of all six areas — least local Thailand experience
  • High season (Nov–Mar) brings real traffic and tourist density on the main roads
  • Gated communities can feel insular if you're not actively social
  • Construction continues in some pockets — worth checking the immediate neighbours of any home
  • Less authentic Thai food than Rawai or Chalong — cafés outnumber local kitchens
