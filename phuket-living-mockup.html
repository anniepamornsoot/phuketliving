Daily life

What living here
actually feels like.

Morning routine

Walk or drive to Rawai seafront for coffee and a local breakfast. Grab fresh fish from the pier market. Very few tourists this early — it's genuinely local.

Schooling

No international schools in Rawai itself — the closest are about 15–20 minutes away. Manageable with a car, but worth knowing before committing.

Getting around

You'll need a car or scooter. Rawai isn't walkable to supermarkets, but Nai Harn beach and Chalong pier are within 10 minutes.

Healthcare

Chalong Hospital is 10 minutes away. Bangkok Hospital Phuket is around 25 minutes. Adequate for day-to-day, with solid options for anything more serious.

Weekends

Nai Harn beach, Windmill viewpoint, Promthep Cape, diving trips from Chalong pier. Some of the best sunsets in Phuket — without the crowds.

The honest picture

What Rawai gets
right — and what it doesn't.

What works well

Some of the best sea views and sunsets in Phuket

Noticeably quieter than the north — you feel the difference immediately

Strong local market scene and authentic Thai restaurants

Lower rents than Bangtao for equivalent space

Growing expat community with a more grounded, less resort-y feel

What to consider

No international schools in the area — a 15–20 min daily drive is required

Not walkable — a car or scooter is essential, not optional

Fewer Western restaurants and supermarkets than Bangtao

Further from the airport (~45 min) — relevant if you travel frequently

Quick answers

Questions Annie gets
asked every week.

Is Phuket safe for families with young children?

+

Yes — Phuket is generally very safe for families. Road safety is the most relevant concern. Most expat families with young kids drive cars rather than scooters. Neighbourhoods like Bangtao and Cherngtalay are quiet and low-risk day-to-day.

Do I need to speak Thai to live comfortably in Phuket?

+

No. English is widely spoken in the areas where most expats live — in shops, restaurants, hospitals, and most services you'll use day-to-day.

How far in advance should I start planning the move?

+

For families with school-age children, 6–12 months is ideal — school enrollment and waiting lists require lead time. For couples without children or retirees, 3–6 months is usually enough.

Should I rent first before deciding where to buy?

+

Almost always, yes. Renting for 6–12 months lets you experience an area through different seasons, understand your actual routine, and make a much more informed purchase decision.

What's the best time of year to visit before relocating?

+

November to April is peak season — dry, sunny, and the easiest time to experience Phuket at its best. If you want to test how you handle the low season, come in August or September during the monsoon.
