Areas/Chalong

Area guide · South-central Phuket

Chalong — local, affordable, sea on its doorstep.

Phuket's southern alternative to Kathu — similarly affordable, similarly close to a hospital, but with Chalong Bay three minutes away and a much slower pace. The most genuinely Thai of the six areas, with Big Buddha on the hill, Wat Chalong on the corner, and the boats heading out to the islands at sunrise.

Annie's honest take

"Chalong is for families who want value without giving up the water entirely. The trade-off is the school commute — Bangtao's cluster is twenty minutes away — but if your kids are pre-school or grown, it's hard to fault. And you'll eat better Thai food here than in any other area on this list."

Daily life

What living here actually feels like.

Not the brochure version. The actual rhythm of a Tuesday in Chalong — what you do, where you go, what's in walking distance and what isn't.

Morning routine

Coffee at one of the small cafés along Chao Fa Road, fresh fish at the pier market, breakfast at one of dozens of local Thai stalls. Mornings here are properly Thai — boats heading out at five, fishermen back by ten, the temple busy with monks doing rounds. Easily the most authentic morning of the six areas.

Schooling

Headstart International is closest at 18 minutes, with the rest of the south cluster within 22–25. Workable for a school-run routine, but worth being honest: this isn't a five-minute drop-off area. Most Chalong families have either pre-school children, grown ones, or have made peace with the commute.

Getting around

A car is essential, but Chalong's flatter than Nai Harn and journeys feel shorter. The pier, the hospital, the temple, the supermarket — all within five minutes of most homes. A scooter genuinely covers most daily errands; the car comes out for the school run and rainy days.

Healthcare

Chalong Hospital is eight minutes for routine care, and Bangkok Hospital Phuket is fifteen. Healthcare access is genuinely strong here — better than Rawai or Nai Harn — and it's a real factor for families with young children or for anyone planning to stay long-term.

Weekends

Big Buddha at sunrise, Wat Chalong at almost any time, day trips out of the pier to Coral Island, Racha, or Phi Phi (the boats start at seven). Rawai beach is five minutes south, Nai Harn twelve. Phuket Town is twenty north for markets, dinner, and Sino-Portuguese architecture. Genuinely well-positioned for weekend variety.

Nearest schools

School options from Chalong.

No international schools in Chalong itself. Headstart International is the closest, with the rest of the south cluster within twenty-five minutes. Drive times are off-peak.

The honest picture

What Chalong gets right — and what it doesn't.

Every area has trade-offs. These are Chalong's, written plainly, so you can decide on the basis of what's actually true rather than what sounds good in a brochure.

What works well

  • Chalong Bay and pier are three minutes — daily access to boats, diving, and sunset trips out
  • Big Buddha and Wat Chalong (Phuket's most important temple) are on your doorstep
  • Cheaper than Bangtao or Cherngtalay for similar space — comparable to Kathu
  • Strong Thai community feel, real markets, easily the best authentic Thai food on this list
  • Chalong Hospital is 8 min away, Bangkok Hospital Phuket within 15
  • More mixed local-expat character than Kathu — feels less "in transition"
  • Excellent base for diving and island life — boats leave from your front door

What to consider

  • 18–25 min to international schools — workable but not ideal for school-age families
  • Chalong Bay isn't a swim beach — for that you drive 5 min south to Rawai or 12 to Nai Harn
  • The pier area gets tourist boat traffic — busy mornings on the main road
  • Fewer Western shops and services than Kathu (Kathu has more retail)
  • Some lower streets see brief monsoon flooding — worth checking elevation of any home
  • Less developed luxury market — fewer high-end villas if that's what you're after
  • Less walkable to Western-style cafés or co-working than Bangtao or Cherngtalay
